Swift Soundscape

It's Taylor Swift's World. We're Just Living In It.

Born in 1989 with an early affinity to music, Taylor Alison Swift signed a 13-year record deal at the mere age of 15. One year later, in 2006, Swift released her debut, self-titled album — Taylor Swift.

As TIME's 2023 Person of the Year, Swift "feels like [this year is] the breakthrough moment of her career, happening at 33."

Nearly two decades since the release of her debut album, which spent 24 weeks at number one on Top Country Albums and received the coveted Album of the Year Grammy nomination, how can Swift still be climbing to the peak of her career?

Swift is the most streamed artist on Spotify and Apple Music, the highest-grossing female performer ever, and the first (estimated) billionaire with music as a main source of income.

This year, she kicked off the North and South American legs of her Eras Tour — a 3 hour and 45 minute concert of 44 songs from the different eras of her life. The Eras Tour shifted economies, with Swift selling out 70k+ seat stadiums across multiple nights and garnering a shout-out from the Federal Reserve as a major contributing factor to local economy booms.

The mania around Swift is rooted in her connection to her fans. Taylor Swift is beloved (and even that feels like an understatement) by her fanbase of Swifties — in fact, at Lumen Field in Seattle, WA, fans cheered so loud that seismographs registered the roar of the crowd as a 2.3 magnitude earthquake.

Her accolades, both impressive and extensive, are somehow only the tip of the iceberg.

This year, more than ever, it feels like Taylor Swift is the music industry.

Who's Taylor Swift anyway?

In 2018, upon the completion of her original deal, Swift signed a new record deal with Universal Music Group, one she hoped would give her more creative freedom over her work.

Under this deal, Swift has released 8 albums, including 4 re-recordings of her previous work and 4 original albums. She has released more albums in the past five years than she did in the first fifteen years of her career.

Swift attributes this to the trust and freedom she has under with her new label, where her decisions are welcomed and encouraged.

Musical Globetrotter

Over the span of her career, Taylor Swift has garnered an impressive fan base of Swifties, who jump at the chance to see Swift in concert. In 2022, when tickets went on sale for Swift's Eras Tour, nearly 5 million fans swarmed Ticketmaster and completely crashed the site, multiple times. Fans waited in online queues of nearly 15,000+ people for hours just hoping to get a chance to try and buy tickets. Tickets on the resale market sold for as high as $20,000.

One of our group members personally waited 11 hours on the Ticketmaster website and walked around campus all day with her laptop open so as not to lose her place.



The Eras Tour is the highest grossing tour of all time, and it's not even halfway done. It will continue throughout the entirety of 2024, exploring multiple cities in in Europe, Asia, Australia, and North America (again).

Prior to her Eras Tour, Taylor has toured in 21 different countries, though a majority of her shows have been in the United States.

Swift is known for her autobiographical storytelling (though her pandemic albums, folklore and evermore were an unexpected and sharp departure from this pattern). At a young age, Swift's family moved from Reading, PA to Nashville, TN in hopes of helping her kickstart her music career. Having spent the early part of her career as an incredibly successful country artist, she made the unprecedented decision in 2014 to fully transition to the pop genre.


In her 1989 (Taylor's Version) prologue, she writes, "When I was 24, I decided to completely reinvent myself." She did this by chopping her long curly hair, moving to New York City, and writing one of the most awarded pop albums of all time — 1989. This era for Swift was defined by paparazzi photos in the streets of New York City and girl groups, and it reflected in the music she wrote.

More than any other place, Taylor Swift references New York in her lyrics. There's the obvious "Welcome to New York," but songs like "Mean," "Come Back," "Holy Ground," "False God," "Daylight" and "Hoax" all include a generic New York reference as well.

Swift's mark as a musician has always been the fact that she is a songwriter on all of her songs. Following her 2010 Album of the Year Grammy win for her album Fearless, critics claimed that she would not have been able to win without the help of her co-writers. In response, Swift penned her next album, Speak Now, entirely by herself.


Swift has an uncanny ability to capture stories and build narrative arcs. She writes lyrics from the most vulnerable and honest parts of herself, and somehow, it speaks directly to the hearts and experiences of her fans. Known for both addicting pop radio production and a heartwrenching power ballad, Swift creates new worlds for her listeners to sink into, and she often does this by leveraging literary devices to engage various themes.
